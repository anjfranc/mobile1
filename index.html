All About Kerala

Kerala is a coastal state in the country of India, located in the southwestern area of the country. The state was officially founded on November 1st 1956, nine years after the independence of India. With an estimated population of 35 million people, the majority speak its official language of Malayalam. Currently, the state is divided into 14 districts with the state capital being Thiruvananthapuram (pronounced teeuh·roo·vuhn-uhn-tha·puh-ruhm), also known as Trivandrum (pronounced tree-van-drum). Likewise, Kerala’s state animal is the elephant which is seen at several festivals and celebrations. Furthermore, its state fruit is the delicious jackfruit.

The region of Kerala is one that was and continues to be famous among foreign countries due to its exotic spices, such as pepper, cardamom, and cloves. In addition, a unique fact about Kerala is that the state’s beautiful and awe-striking scenery has led it to being called “God’s Own Country”. Moreover, Kerala is also named after its numerous coconut plantations and can translate to the land of coconut trees. Approximately, 1,902,711 acres of Kerala’s land is utilized for coconut plantation. Overall, Kerala is a state with so much to do and see!
